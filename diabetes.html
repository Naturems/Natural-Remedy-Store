Diabetes, its Causes, Symptoms, and Remedy

Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs either when the pancreas does not produce enough hormone that regulates blood sugar or when the body cannot effectively use the hormones produced by the pancreas. These two reasons gave rise to the two major types of diabetes: the Type 1; the pancreas produces no or very low quantity of this hormone, the Type 2: the pancreas produces the necessary hormones but the body cannot make use of these hormones. These results in the build up of extra sugar in your bloodstream. With a build up of extra or excess sugar in the bloodstream, one is at the dangers of having serious complications which may result in unpalatable consequences. Another type of diabetes is Gestational diabetes which is liable to develop during pregnancy. Hyperglycemia, or raised blood sugar, is a common effect of uncontrolled diabetes and over time leads to serious damage to many of the body's systems, especially the nerves and blood vessels.

How to Treat Diabetes

    Treatment of diabetes involves diet and physical activity along with lowering of blood glucose and the levels of other known risk factors that damage blood vessels. Here is a list of what to do to manage diabetes:
  • Exercise regularly
  • Always be hydrated
  • Reduce/manage stress
  • Take time to rest
  • Reduce carbohydrate intake
  • Eat more Fibre: Vegetables, Fruits, legumes e.t.c
  • Eat food with no glucose outcome: Beans, Oats, Wheat, Barley, e.t.c
  • Weight control or maintainance: Check your weight regularly and work to reduce it
