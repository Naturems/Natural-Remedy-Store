Fibroid, Its Causes, Symptoms and Remedy

Fibroid

Fibroids are smooth muscle cells (tumors) made of and fibrous connective tissue that grows in the uterus. They are sometimes called Uterine Fibroid for this. It can be thought of as Benign tumor containing fibrous tissue. They are mostly known to appear during childbearing years. Often times, uterus fibroid shows no sign of their presence to the host because most times, they cause no symptoms especially when they are very little in size. It is the most common tumor of the reproductive system and are most often discovered during a routine pelvic exam. There are different sizes associated with fibroid growth,from very tiny seedlings which are not visible to the eyes, to large sizes that can affect the structure and size of the uterus. It can be just one or in any number.

In extreme cases, multiple fibroids can expand the uterus so much that it reaches the rib cage and can add weight. The main types of fibroids are: Subserosal which sometimes have a stalk that attaches to the uterus and can grow large, Intramural: which are found in the uterus muscular all, and Submucosal; are very rare, they grow openly and may also include a stalk. Most doctors agrees that fibroids cells are formed as a result of anomalies in a stem cell in the smooth muscular tissue of the uterus (myometrium). This anomaly is when a single cell divides repeatedly continuously until it creates a firm rubbery mass of tissue that is different from the nearby tissue.

Symptoms of Fibroid

    Often times, many women with fibroid rarely notice any sign of its presence. But as it grows and increases in numbers, some of the noticable symptoms may include;

  • Heavy or prolonged menstrual periods
  • Frequent urination
  • Difficulty emptying the bladder
  • Constipation
  • Pain during intercourse
  • Difficulty getting pregnant
  • Backache and/or leg pains

Causes of Fibroid

    The causes of Uterine fibroids are still not clearly ascertained, but some lab researches has pointed to some factors such as these;

  • Changes in human genetics: A large number of fibroid cells contains genes that are different from the typical uterine muscle cells.
  • Hormones: A larger quantity of the hormones that causes uterine lining development during menstrual cycle, estrogen and progesterone, are found in fibroid cells.
  • Age: A large number of women within the ages 30-45 are especially and up to menopause are often diagnosed with the cases of fibroid. In most cases also, fibroids are less likely to be found at menopause ages and they tend to shrink even when found.
  • Hereditary: Having history of fibroid in ones family increases the chances of having it. As a lady, if your mother has history with fibroid in her life, then, there is a higher chance of having them yourself.
